Saudi Performing Arts

Saudi performing arts reflect the richness of the Kingdom's cultural heritage, combining music, poetry, and movement. This page highlights some of the most well-known traditional performances that continue to play an important role in celebrations and social life.

Al-Ardah

A traditional Saudi performance known as Najdi Ardah, performed during national events and celebrations. It symbolizes strength and unity and is recognized by UNESCO as intangible cultural heritage.

Al-Samri

A popular folk music performance that originated in Al-Qassim and spread to different regions. It combines singing, poetry, and rhythmic movement.

Yanbuawi Art

A coastal musical art from Yanbu, characterized by fast rhythms. It is part of traditional sea songs performed by sailors.

Southern Ardah

A traditional dance from southern Saudi Arabia, performed during celebrations and weddings, expressing bravery and pride.

Al-Zamil

A group performance from Asir and Najran where participants stand in rows and chant poetry together during special occasions.

Qasimi Dance

A traditional singing dance performed in western regions, known for its unique Hijazi rhythm and musical style.

Al-Dahha

A northern war dance performed by men, featuring chanting and strong rhythmic movements that reflect courage and unity.

Al-Majroor

A folkloric dance popular in western Saudi Arabia, especially in Taif, known for its rhythmic drumming and group performance.
